State #48

Arizona was introduced into the US on February 14, 1912 as the 48th state. Upon introduction, Arizona was used for the immense copper found within its borders and continues to lead the nation in copper production. Home to National Parks such as the Grand Canyon and the Petrified Forest, Arizona can be dated back over 100,000 years and provide proof that we were below sea level once upon a time. In addition to National Parks, Arizona houses some of the world's largest formations of speleothems (minerals mixed with water) in its Kartchner Caverns and Colossal Caves. For more information on these and more of Arizona's beautiful sites, visit our Main Attractions page.
